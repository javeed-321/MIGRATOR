# Types of Cart Promotion

There are five types of cart promotions available. You can select a promotion based on whether you want to target specific customers, specific behaviors, or the general public.

# 1. Loyalty cart promotion

*Promotion for selected audience without earnings.*

These promotions target specific customer groups based on their purchase history, preferences, or tier status.

* **How it works:** The system does not issue these to a customer manually. If a customer fits the criteria (for example, belongs to the "Gold" tier or has bought coffee five times), they automatically receive the benefit when they shop.
* **Best for:** Personalization and retention.
* **Example:** Gold-tier customers receive a 60% discount on coffee.

# 2. Loyalty earning cart promotion

*Promotion for selected audience with activity or milestone earning.*

These promotions are **issued** to a customer only when they complete a specific milestone or loyalty-related activity.

* **How it works:** An action, such as registering an account, updating a profile, or completing a specific number of transactions, triggers the benefit, such as a coupon or points.
* **Best for:** Providing benefits for non-transactional behaviors and data collection.
* **Example:** A brand encourages customers to update their profile by offering a flat 15% discount on their next purchase.

# 3. POS cart promotion

*Promotion not restricted to any specific customer set.*

POS cart promotions are general discounts available to all customers who transact at the Point of Sale (POS).

* **How it works:** These apply automatically at checkout based on the cart contents.
* **Best for:** Clearance sales, weekend specials, or general inventory movement.
* **Example:** Clearance sale for a specific product category (Wafers). For example "Buy wafers for $2.90 and save $1.50. Available on weekends between 10 AM and 6 PM."

# 4. Rewards cart promotion

*Promotion is earned by a customer on an external trigger like reward buying.*

A rewards cart promotion is a redeemable item in your **Rewards Catalog**.

* **How it works:** Customers use their accrued loyalty points to "purchase" this promotion. Once redeemed, the promotion logic applies a discount to their cart.
* **Best for:** "Burning" liability (points) and increasing engagement with the rewards program.
* **Example**: Redeem 500 points to unlock a "Buy 1 Get 1 Free" voucher.

# 5. Code-linked cart promotion

*Generate promo codes that are not restricted to any type of customer.*

Code-linked cart promotions are tied to specific physical or digital promo codes and are valid for anyone who possesses the code, regardless of their loyalty status.

* **How it works:** The customer must manually enter the code at checkout. Brands commonly use these in influencer marketing or partner campaigns.
* **Key Rules:**
  * Each promo code can be limited to **one use per customer**.
  * Codes expire automatically when the promotion ends.
  * Brands can set **Global Limits** such as limiting total redemptions to the first 1,000 users.
* **Example (Influencer Marketing):**
  * A brand provides an influencer named **Mansi** with the promo code HOLIMAN100.
  * She shares it with her followers.
  * The brand sets a limit so the code is only valid for the first 1,000 users who redeem it.
